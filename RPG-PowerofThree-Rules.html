Character Creation

1Choose Your Race: A Halfling can be a hardened warrior, and a Dwarf can master the arcane arts. You are not restricted.

2Allocate 300 XP: You receive a starting pool of 300 XP to purchase your initial skills. This limited pool forces you to make hard choices about your survival.

3Calculate Derived Traits: Determine your Base Defense (from Agility and Armor) and your Total Wounds (Base Racial Wounds + Physique/Skill bonuses).

4Equip Your Hero: Use your starting gold to purchase armaments and survival gear.

The Hero's Resources

Luck & Fortune DEfying Fate

Luck: Roll 1d10 at the start of each session. You may spend up to half (rounded up) of your total luck per single roll to achieve success or prevent a critical failure.

Fortune (Rerolls): For each point in the Fortune skill, you are granted one reroll per game session.

Action Points (AP) The Economy of Combat

Every hero starts with a base of 1 Action Point per turn. You can increase your total AP pool by investing in the Quickness skill or purchasing AP directly with XP.

  • Attacking: Swinging a 1H blade costs 1 Action, while firing a Heavy Crossbow requires 3 Actions.
  • Casting: The complexity of a spell dictates how many actions it takes to invoke.
  • Utility: Activating Focus, breaking free from vines, or drinking a potion typically costs 1 Action.

Anatomy of a Turn

"Time on the battlefield is measured in heartbeats and drawn steel."

↑ Return to Index
Initiative & Surprise

Combat begins by rolling a single 1d10 vs the DM's 1d10. Add the Agility stat of the fastest player vs. the Agility stat of the fastest monster to the respective rolls.

Surprise: If attacking from a hidden position, the attacking party gains Advantage. An entirely undetected ambushing side gains Advantage (Roll 2d10, drop lowest) and a flat +2 to their result.

Phases & Movement

During your party's turn, you may move up to your race's distance. You can make a partial move, attack, then finish your move.

Sprint (1 Action): Move an additional 15 Ft (or ½ your normal max move), but you lose all normal attacks this turn.

Terrain Penalties: Uneasy (-5 Ft), Rough (-10 Ft), Risky (-20 Ft).

Core Mechanics

"The dice govern the chaos of the unknown. Learn to master them, or be broken by them."

↑ Return to Index
The Core Roll (To-Hit)

To score a hit against an enemy target, or to successfully cast a spell, you roll a 1d10. You must achieve a number equal to or higher than the target's Defense value (or the spell's Target Number). You then apply any natural characteristics or skill bonuses to your result.

Advantage & Disadvantage

When the odds of combat shift drastically, the dice reflect it:

  • Advantage: Roll an additional 1d10. Evaluate both dice and select the highest score as your result.
  • Disadvantage: Roll an additional 1d10, but you are forced to take the lesser score of the two as your result.
Astounding Success (Exploding Dice) NATURAL 10

If your To-Hit roll is an unmodified 10, you immediately roll a second 1d10. If this second roll also meets or beats the target's Defense, you are granted an additional 1d10 to calculate the total wounds caused.

Chaining Successes: If subsequent rolls result in another 10, you may roll as many times as possible in a row to continuously increase the wounds caused!

The Flub (Catastrophic Failure) NATURAL 1

If your To-Hit result is an unmodified 1, it is an automatic miss. You may spend a point of Luck to avoid further penalties. If you cannot (or choose not to), you must roll a second 1d10.

If the second roll is also a 1, you suffer a catastrophic failure. The DM will present a test to avoid dropping your weapon, striking an ally, or suffering a magical miscast.

Melee Combat

"The clash of steel and the breaking of bone. In the melee, only the swift and the brutal survive."

Weapon Proficiencies

You must select a single weapon proficiency for your first point in the Combat (Melee) skill. Using a weapon you are not proficient with imposes Disadvantage on both To-Hit and To-Wound rolls.

  • Blunt: Club, Mace, Flail, Morning Star, Quarterstaff, Warhammers.
  • Edged: Axes, Swords, Claymores, Cutlasses, Falchions.
  • Piercing: Daggers, Lances, Pikes, Rapiers, Spears.
  • Polearm: Awl Pikes, Halberds, Staffs.
Weapon Stances & Choices
  • 1H-Weapon w/ Shield: Balanced defense and offense. No penalty To-Hit or To-Wound.
  • 2H-Weapon / Polearm: Devastating but unwieldy. Suffer Disadvantage To-Hit, but gain Advantage To-Wound.
  • Dual-Wield: Grants 1 additional attack per turn. Suffer Disadvantage To-Hit and gain no Advantage To-Wound. (Requires two Small/Medium weapons, or one Large and one Small/Medium).
  • Unarmed: Desperate brawling. Grants 1 additional attack. Suffer Disadvantage To-Wound against Leather armor or better.
  • Mounted: High ground advantage. Gain Advantage on To-Hit and To-Wound against smaller or equal-sized unmounted targets.
Tactical Situations

Backstab: Striking an unaware foe from the shadows deals 1d10 x 2 wounds. Requires a successful Stealth vs Awareness test.

Outnumbered: Swarming your prey. Outnumbering a target at least 2-to-1 in melee grants Advantage To-Hit.

Ranks: Weapons like spears allow To-Hit attempts from a 2nd-line rank with no penalty.

Disengage: Fleeing combat. Roll 1d10 + Agility Bonus + Skill Points to set the Defense Value (DV) the enemy must beat for their free strike against you.

CHAPTER V

Ranged Combat

"Death from afar. A steady hand and a keen eye can end a battle before the enemy even draws their blades."

↑ Return to Index
Weapon Proficiencies (Ranged)

You must select a proficiency for your first point in the Combat (Ranged) skill. Non-proficient weapons impose Disadvantage To-Hit.

  • Bow: Short Bow, Longbow, Recurve, Elven Bow.
  • Crossbow: Light, Standard, and Heavy Crossbows.
  • Throwing: Javelins, Knives, Axes, Spears. (Add Physique bonus to Wound rolls).
  • Black Powder: Pistols, Rifles, Blunderbuss.
Aiming & Loading

The Aim Action: Spend 1 Action to aim. This grants Advantage To-Hit and To-Wound on your next turn and extends your maximum range by 50%.

Loading & Readying: Ranged weapons with high action costs (2+) represent the time needed to reload. A pre-loaded weapon requires only 1 Action for the first shot.

Line of Sight & Cover

Cover: Hiding behind trees or walls grants the target Disadvantage To-Hit against you. Targeting a specific individual in a group imposes a -1 penalty per obstructing target.

Firing into Melee: Shooting into engaged combat imposes Disadvantage To-Hit (unless the target is 2x the size of your ally). A miss does not hit your ally; the penalty simply represents you aiming safely.

CHAPTER VI

The Arcane & Divine

"Magic is the blood of the world. Those who learn to manipulate it hold the power of gods."

↑ Return to Index
To-Cast & Invoke

Magic relies on meeting a specific Target Number (TN). Roll 1d10 and apply your Intellect or Willpower bonuses. If the total meets or exceeds the TN, the spell is successful. A natural 10 grants an additional 1d10 to boost potency.

The Surge

You may forgo your normal turn (attacks/casts) to build raw magical energy. You may still use your move action. This grants you Advantage on your next To-Cast/Invoke roll.

Casting Mechanics

Into Melee: There is no penalty to cast when targeting units actively engaged in combat.

Resisting Magic: Targets may make Agility or Willpower tests to reduce wounds or evade effects. The DV they must beat is equal to your total To-Cast roll.

Miscast & Wrath: A natural 1 on a casting attempt is a Flub. Failing to spend Luck to prevent it results in a disastrous magical backfire on the Miscast/Wrath table.

The Path of Power & Scaling

Once you allocate 6 points in Druidic, Faith, or Wizardry skills, you must choose a specialized Path, unlocking unique magic restricted from generalists.

Scaling: You may spend additional XP during character progression to permanently increase the number of targets affected by your spells or extend their 1d10 turn duration.

CHAPTER VII

Status & Conditions

"The battlefield is cruel. Blood loss, terror, and broken bones will kill you just as fast as an enemy's blade."

↑ Return to Index

I. Physical Trauma

These conditions represent ongoing damage to the body. Effects trigger at the start of the afflicted creature's turn.

Bleeding (Stacking)

Lose 1d10 Wounds per stack at the start of your turn. Stacks are removed via a successful Physique Test (costs 1 Action) or magical healing.

Poison (Minor/Major)

Minor: Lose 1 Wound per turn.
Major: Lose Dd10 Wounds per turn. Pass a Willpower Test at the end of your turn to remove.

Disease

Lose 1d10 Wounds per turn. Additionally, suffer a -1 penalty to a random characteristic every 24 hours until cleansed by magic.

II. Tactical Penalties

These conditions restrict your movement and available Actions on the battlefield.

Stunned

You immediately lose 1 Action Point (AP). If you have no actions remaining this turn, you lose 1 AP from your next turn instead.

Prone

You have been knocked off your feet. You suffer Disadvantage on all To-Hit rolls, and enemies gain Advantage to hit you in melee. Standing up costs 1 Action.

Pinned / Entangled / Frozen

Pinned/Entangled: You cannot move. Attacks and casts are made at Disadvantage. Pass a Physique or Agility test to break free.

Frozen: Encased in ice. Cannot move or take physical actions. You may only attempt Willpower-based casts or mental actions.

III. Mental & Sensory

These conditions target the mind and perception.

Fear (X)

Must pass a Willpower Test with a penalty equal to the Fear Rating (X). On a failure, you must spend all actions moving away from the source.

Blinded

All Melee and Ranged attacks are made at Disadvantage. Your Defense rating is reduced to your Armor value only.

Dismayed / Allure

Dismayed: You lose your Move action on your next turn and cannot perform Reaction actions (like Parry or Dodge).

Allure: Treat the source as a trusted ally. You will not attack them. Ends if attacked by the source.

IV. The Rule of Three & Unconsciousness

Overwhelmed

If you are ever afflicted by three or more different conditions at once, you are Overwhelmed. You cannot take any Reaction Actions (Dodge, Parry, Counter) until at least one condition is cleared.

Unconscious

Triggered at 0 Wounds. If healed to 1+ Wound, you move at ½ speed and require a Willpower test to perform an attack or complex action.

CHAPTER VIII

Growth & Advancement

"Experience is the best teacher, though its lessons are often paid for in blood."

↑ Return to Index
Earning Experience

In the Path of the Unknown, characters do not level up in the traditional sense. They earn Experience Points (XP) through trials, combat, and roleplay. The DM typically awards between 25 and 75 XP per session based on the magnitude of the party's achievements.

The Compounding Cost

Mastery is not easily attained and uses a Compounding XP Cost. The first rank is affordable, but subsequent ranks become increasingly expensive.

  • Rank 1: 15 XP
  • Rank 2: 30 XP
  • Rank 3: 45 XP
  • Rank 4: 60 XP

*Specializations or high-tier Masteries may have higher base costs but follow the same compounding logic.

Characteristic Advancement

Your six Core Characteristics are not static. For every 3 Skill Points allocated into skills belonging to a specific characteristic (Agility, Awareness, Charisma, Intellect, Physique, Willpower), that parent characteristic automatically increases by 1 point. Your stats represent the sum total of your specific training.

Skill Tiers & Classless Freedom

Requirements: Most skills are available to all, but Advanced Skills and Masteries require a foundation (e.g., you cannot master Wizardry without first understanding Knowledge).

Freedom: You are never locked into a path. A Wizard who finds a magical greatsword can choose to spend their next 100 XP on Melee proficiencies to become a battle-mage.

Ballistics & Penetration
  • Piercing Weapons: Black powder and Crossbows ignore the listed amount of Wound Mitigation. If the mitigation is lower than the piercing value, the extra damage is not added.

The Laws of Runecraft
  • The Rule of Three: No single object (weapon, armor piece, or talisman) may bear more than 3 total runes.
  • Mastery Limit: An object may only bear 1 Master Rune at any given time.
  • No Duplication: You cannot carve the same rune twice onto the same object to double its effects. Multiplier runes (like Might and Skaif) do not stack with each other.

Primary Skills Compendium

This grimoire details the foundational skills of survival, combat, and arcane mastery. These abilities form the core of your character's capabilities before advancing into specialized vocations.

Primary Skills (A-Z)

Acute Senses Power of Three: +1 Awareness per 3 points. | Cost: Halfling (15), Dwarf/Elf (20), Human (25)

The world speaks in whispers, shifting shadows, and the metallic tang of drawn steel. While others are blind to the subtle warnings of the environment, your instincts are honed to a razor's edge.

Your heightened sensitivity to sight, sound, smell, and taste grants you a crucial edge in survival. When actively searching, or when the GM determines a hidden threat (such as an ambush or backstab) is imminent, you make a sensory test by rolling 1d10 + your points in this skill.

Athletics Power of Three: +1 Max Wounds per point. | Cost: Elf/Human (15), Dwarf/Halfling (20)

The body is the ultimate weapon, forged not in the armory, but through exhaustion, sweat, and sheer physical exertion. You have conditioned your muscles to push past mortal limits, turning obstacles into mere stepping stones.

Your physical conditioning grants you superior mobility and endurance.

  • Physical Feats: When attempting to leap wide chasms, scale sheer surfaces, or push your running speed past natural limits, you roll 1d10 + your points in this skill to overcome the GM's Difficulty Value (DV).
  • Hardened Physique: For every point invested in Athletics, your maximum Wound threshold permanently increases by 1.
  • Lethal Force (Threshold 3+): Once you have invested 3 or more points into Athletics, your physical power translates directly into ranged combat. You inflict additional wounds (amount determined by weapon/GM) on successful strikes with ranged weapons.
Authority Power of Three: +1 Charisma per 3 points. | Cost: Dwarf/Elf/Human (15), Halfling (20)

Some lead by the grace of kings, but you lead by the weight of your presence. Whether through the cold promise of violence or an unshakable aura of command, you force the world to bow to your will.

You may attempt to force an individual or a group to act against their will, surrender, or comply with a command. The Clash of Wills: You roll 1d10 + your points in this skill to set the Difficulty Value (DV). The target must immediately roll 1d10 + their Will Power characteristic to resist. If they fail to meet or exceed your DV, they must comply with your demand to the best of their ability.

Charm Power of Three: +1 Charisma per 3 points. | Cost: Halfling/Human (5), Elf/Dwarf (10)

In a harsh world, a silver tongue and a disarming smile can open doors that lockpicks and broadswords cannot. You weave words and presence into a subtle snare, drawing others into your confidence.

You may attempt to lure, pacify, or gain the immediate favor of a target through social grace, flattery, or sheer magnetism.

  • The Allure Test: You roll 1d10 + your points in this skill to set the Difficulty Value (DV). The target must roll to resist this DV using either 1d10 + Awareness (to spot manipulation) or 1d10 + Will Power (to resist emotional sway).
  • Social Grace: Simply having this skill unlocked grants its point value as a direct bonus to any general Charisma tests called for by the GM.
Combat (Melee) Requirement: Purchased per weapon category. | Cost: Dwarf (25), Elf/Human (30), Halfling (35)

A weapon is not an extension of the arm until blood, sweat, and repetition forge the bond. Without discipline, a blade is just a heavy piece of iron waiting to get you killed.

True martial prowess is earned, not given. By default, attempting to attack with any melee weapon you are not proficient in incurs Disadvantage on the to-hit roll.

  • Weapon Specialization: Purchasing a point in this skill unlocks proficiency for one specific category of melee combat.
  • Mastery: Once unlocked for a specific category, you permanently lose the disadvantage penalty when attacking with weapons that fall under that classification.

Proficiencies Available: Axes & Cleavers, Flexible/Chain Weapons, Hammers & Maces, Polearms & Spears, Swords & Daggers, Unarmed Combat.

Combat (Mounted) Prerequisites: Ride (1), Combat Proficiency | Cost: 100 XP (All Races)

True dominance on the battlefield is achieved when the beast beneath you and the steel in your hand move as one singular, devastating force. To fight from the saddle is to command the tides of war.

You have mastered the chaotic art of fighting while controlling a charging mount. Your available attacks are dictated by your mount's movement during the turn:

  • Stationary/Engaged: If you ride up to a target and halt your mount's movement, you may execute your full number of standard melee or ranged attacks.
  • Hit-and-Run: If your mount is actively moving past an enemy, you are restricted to a single melee strike or a single ranged attack for the turn.
  • The Cavalry Charge: When wielding a Lance and striding directly toward your target, you may execute a single, devastating attack using the mount's momentum.

Note: Attempting to fight from a mount without this skill results in severe penalties, or outright failure, at the GM's discretion.

Combat (Ranged) Requirement: Purchased per weapon category. | Cost: Elf (25), Dwarf/Halfling/Human (30)

True marksmanship is a marriage of breath, tension, and geometry. To loose an arrow without understanding the wind or the draw weight is simply wasting wood and steel.

Firing a projectile with lethal accuracy requires specialized muscle memory. By default, attempting to attack with any ranged weapon you are not proficient in incurs Disadvantage on the to-hit roll.

  • Weapon Specialization: Purchasing a point in this skill unlocks proficiency for one specific category of ranged combat.
  • Mastery: Once unlocked for a specific category, you permanently lose the disadvantage penalty when attacking with weapons that fall under that classification.

Proficiencies Available: Bows, Crossbows, Firearms, Throwing Weapons.

Counter Requirements: 1 Action, Melee Weapon | Cost: 30 XP (All Races) | Max: Agility

A clumsy strike is a dead man's invitation. You have trained your reflexes to exploit the exact moment your enemy overextends, punishing their hesitation with a blindingly fast riposte.

You turn an enemy's failure into a lethal opening.

  • The Opening: If an opponent attacking you in melee rolls an unmodified, natural 1 on their to-hit roll, you may immediately execute a reactionary strike.
  • The Riposte: Instead of calculating standard weapon wounds, this lightning-fast jab, pommel strike, or slice inflicts 1d10 + your points in this skill in immediate wounds.
  • Action Economy: Executing this counter-attack immediately consumes one of your available actions. If you have already exhausted your action pool for the round, you cannot trigger this skill.
Discipline Power of Three: +1 Awareness per 3 pts. | +1 Max Wounds & +1 Resource per pt. | Cost: Dwarf/Elf/Human (5), Halfling (10)

The mind commands, and the flesh obeys. Through rigorous fasting, relentless meditation, and unbreakable vows, you have forged your body and spirit into a single, hardened vessel.

Your unwavering commitment to your training grants you both physical resilience and spiritual capacity.

  • Fortified Vessel: For every point invested in this skill, your maximum Wound threshold permanently increases by 1.
  • Expanded Mind/Soul: For every point invested, you gain the capacity to hold 1 additional magical or spiritual resource (such as a Blessing, Ki Power, Rune Inscription, or Memorized Spell), depending on your class/training.
Druid (Magical Vocation) Power of Three: +1 Awareness per 3 pts. | Prereqs: Knowledge Druidic (1), Read/Scribe (1)

The world breathes. From the deepest bedrock to the canopy of the ancient wilds, a primal energy flows. You have learned not merely to observe this power, but to channel it, drawing upon the living earth to shape the battlefield.

You unlock the ability to wield Druidic magic, drawing upon natural and planetary energies.

  • Arcane Repertoire: For every point invested in this skill, you permanently unlock and learn one Druidic spell.
  • Restorative Resonance: Your capability to mend flesh is tied to your perception of the natural order. You gain direct bonuses to your healing spells as your core Awareness characteristic increases.
Enraged Power of Three: +1 Will Power per 3 pts. | Cooldown: 2 turns | Cost: Dwarf (30), Elf/Human (35), Halfling (40)

A red haze descends, drowning out fear, fatigue, and reason. There is only the immediate threat, the pounding of your own blood, and the relentless urge to break whatever stands before you.

You push your body past its physical limits, unleashing pure hatred upon your enemies. For the duration of the turn:

  • You gain immunity to fear.
  • You gain +1 extra attack per point you possess in this skill. (All bonus Enraged attacks are rolled with Disadvantage unless mitigated by another skill).
  • Vulnerabilities & Restrictions: You suffer "Tunnel Vision"—you must attack the closest enemy target and cannot voluntarily flee combat. You cannot cast spells, use invocations, or gain the numerical advantage of outnumbering an opponent on normal attacks. You cannot trigger this skill if you are currently Exhausted.
Evaluation (Object) Power of Three: +1 Intellect per 3 pts. | Cost: 10 XP (All Races)

To the untrained eye, a rusted blade is merely scrap, and a dusty tome is just kindling. But to the educated mind, every scratch, rune, and maker's mark tells a story of forgotten ages, hidden wealth, and latent magical danger.

You possess a trained eye for history, craftsmanship, and arcane signatures, allowing you to identify the nature, value, and potential magical properties of artifacts.

  • The Scholar's Eye: By default, attempting to identify or appraise an obscure or magical item without this skill is rolled with Disadvantage. Purchasing your first point in Evaluation permanently removes this penalty.
  • Appraisal Tests: When inspecting an item, you roll 1d10 + your points in this skill to determine how much information you glean against a GM-determined Difficulty Value (DV).
Evasion Power of Three: +1 Defense, +1 WM, +1 Agility per 3 pts. | Cost: Elf/Halfling (15), Human (20), Dwarf (25)

Armor can be pierced, and shields can be splintered, but a blade that meets empty air inflicts no harm. True survival lies in anticipating the strike—whether by dancing out of its path entirely, or shifting just enough to turn a fatal blow into a glancing deflection.

You have developed uncanny physical intuition, allowing you to slip past lethal strikes in the chaos of melee or the hail of ranged fire.

  • The Agility Tax (Defense): Your base Evasion bonus (1 for every 3 points invested) is added to your core Defense. However, this bonus is directly reduced by the innate Agility penalty of your equipped armor. Wearing massive, restrictive steel (like Field Plate) will physically prevent you from dodging, negating this Defense bonus entirely.
  • Rolling with the Punch (Mitigation): Even if your armor is too heavy to physically dodge an attack, your martial training allows you to brace and shift your weight to absorb the impact. Your base Evasion bonus is added directly to your total Wound Mitigation, ignoring your armor's Agility penalty.
Faith Power of Three: +1 Charisma per 3 pts. | Prereqs: Knowledge Religion (1), Read/Scribe (1) | Cost: 20 XP

To believe is to see what others cannot; to act is to manifest the will of the heavens upon the earth. You are not merely a worshiper, but a conduit—a living vessel through which your deity’s grace or wrath flows into the mortal realm.

You unlock the ability to invoke divine blessings granted by your deity.

  • Divine Repertoire: For every point invested in this skill, you permanently unlock and learn one Blessing. (Consult the Blessings table for the XP costs required to further empower these invocations).
  • Invocation Rules: Blessings are utilized through the required action cost specified in the individual Blessing entries.

Note: Losing favor with your deity or violating their core tenets may result in the temporary or permanent loss of these abilities at the GM’s discretion.

Focus (Combat Resource) Power of Three: +1 Awareness & +1 To-Hit per 3 pts. | Action Cost: 1 AP | Cost: 35 XP

The world narrows to a single point. The frantic rhythm of the melee slows as your pulse steadies. You do not merely strike; you choose exactly where the blade will bite and how deep the iron will go.

Your total score in this skill acts as a pool of energy you can expend when you take the "Focus" action.

  • The Tactical Pool: When you Focus before an attack, spell, or healing action, you distribute your Total Focus Points between two effects:
    - Potency (Damage/Healing): Each point adds a +1 flat bonus to the total Wounds dealt or Health restored.
    - Penetration (Armor Pierce): Each point ignores 1 point of the target’s Armor Mitigation.
  • The Precision Threshold: Your mastery of focus naturally guides your hand. For every 3 total points you possess in this skill, you gain a passive +1 bonus to your Hit/Cast roll whenever you utilize the Focus action. This bonus is automatic and does not cost points from your pool.

Tactical Example: A character with 6 points in Focus (+2 to Hit threshold) is facing an armored Iron-Minotaur. They spend 1 AP to Focus. They automatically gain a +2 bonus to their To-Hit roll. They choose to allocate 4 points into Penetration to bypass the beast's armor and 2 points into Potency to ensure the strike is lethal.

Fortitude Power of Three: +1 Physique per 3 pts. | +10 Max Wounds per pt. | Cost: Dwarf (45), Human (50), Elf/Halfling (55)

Some men are like oak; they may be scarred and battered by the storm, but they do not break. You have hardened your very marrow, turning your body into a bastion of endurance that can withstand blows that would fell a lesser being.

Your raw physical endurance is unparalleled, allowing you to absorb massive amounts of punishment.

  • Hardened Frame: For every point invested in this skill, your base maximum Wound threshold permanently increases by 10.
  • Size Synergy: These wounds are added to your base before Size Multipliers are applied. (e.g., A Large creature gains +20 wounds per point instead of +10).
  • Unstoppable Threshold (3+ Points): Characters who push beyond 3 points in Fortitude begin to see compounding returns on their resilience, further increasing wound bonuses as their physical form transcends mortal limits.
Fortune Benefit: +1 Session Reroll per pt. | Cost: 30 XP (All Races)

The gods do not play dice with the universe, but you have learned how to nudge the hand of Fate when she isn’t looking. Whether it is divine favor, a lucky charm, or simply the stubborn refusal to die, things just seem to go your way when it matters most.

You possess a pool of "Fortune" that allows you to defy the odds and rewrite a moment of failure.

  • The Reroll: For every point invested in this skill, you gain one reroll per game session.
  • Usage: You may expend a Fortune point to reroll any 1d10 result (attack, skill test, or saving throw). You must accept the result of the second roll.
  • Dashboard Synergy: Your total Reroll count is a read-only field on your character sheet, automatically calculated by your total investment in this skill.

Note: Fortune points refresh at the start of every new session. Use them wisely—once the well of luck runs dry, you are at the mercy of the dice once more.

There are those who bleed at the first sign of steel, and then there are those who simply refuse to acknowledge the pain. You have pushed your body through countless trials, thickening your skin and hardening your resolve until your very flesh acts as a secondary layer of armor.

You possess a terrifying level of physical grit, allowing you to endure punishment that would incapacitate others.

  • Dense Vitality: For every point invested in this skill, your maximum Wound threshold permanently increases by 2.
  • Natural Mitigation: Your sheer toughness allows you to absorb the force of incoming blows. You gain +1 Wound Mitigation for every 2 points invested in this skill. This bonus applies to wounds caused by melee strikes and standard ranged attacks, acting as a permanent reduction to incoming damage.

Iron Will Power of Three: +1 Will Power per 3 pts. | +2 Wounds per pt. | Cost: Dwarf (25), Human/Elf/Halfling (30)

The flesh is weak, a tapestry of salt and water easily torn. But the will is iron, forged in the fires of conviction. You do not survive because your heart beats; you survive because you refuse to let it stop.

Your mental fortitude translates directly into physical survivability and resistance to external influences.

  • Lifeblood: For every point invested in this skill, your maximum Wound threshold permanently increases by 2.
  • The Veteran's Surge: Upon reaching 3 points in Iron Will, your body undergoes a hardening of spirit, granting a one-time flat bonus of +5 Wounds to your profile.
  • Death’s Door: You possess an uncanny ability to linger at the threshold of the grave. When reduced to 0 Wounds, you gain an extension of 1 turn per point in this skill before succumbing to death.

  • Arcane Resistance: Your hardened mind naturally repels external manipulation. This skill provides a baseline defense against hostile Blessings and Spells (calculated via the Will Power characteristic).

Spiritual energy is not found in the world around you, but within the silent depths of the soul. By channeling this inner fire, you can manifest your will as a physical force, transcending the limits of the mundane body.

You have learned to tap into your inner spiritual reservoir to execute extraordinary feats.

  • The Path of the Monk: Your mastery of Ki allows you to manifest specific powers. You may learn and utilize a total number of Ki powers equal to the points invested in this skill.
  • Physical Fortification: As you refine your spiritual focus, your physical vessel becomes more resilient. For every 3 points invested in this skill, your core Physique characteristic permanently increases by 1.
  • Power Invocation: Ki powers function similarly to Blessings and Spells; they are utilized and tracked according to the rules of your spiritual tradition.
Knowledge Power of Three: +1 Intellect per 3 pts. | Cost: 15 XP (All Races)

A sword can be shattered and gold can be stolen, but a secret once learned is a weapon that never dulls. You have dedicated yourself to the study of the world, cataloging the nuances of history, the patterns of magic, and the behaviors of the beasts that prowl the dark.

This skill is not a singular expertise but a collection of specialized disciplines. Each point invested represents a deep dive into a specific subject.

  • The Record: Each time a point is purchased, you must define the specific area of knowledge gained. This should be recorded on the character profile.
  • Information Gathering: When attempting to recall information, identify a weakness, or translate an ancient text, you roll 1d10 + your points in that specific Knowledge field against the GM’s Difficulty Value (DV).

Common Areas of Knowledge:

  • Bestiary: Weaknesses, habitats, and behaviors of monsters.
  • History: Significant events, noble lineages, and the fall of empires.
  • Lore: Local legends, myths, and rumors.
  • Magic: Theory of the weave, identification of spells, and arcane artifacts.
  • Races: Customs, languages, and physiological traits of various cultures.
  • Religions: Liturgy, deities, and the hierarchy of the priesthood.
Light-Soul (Faith-Bound Skill) Power of Three: +1 Charisma & +1 WM (vs Evil) per 3 pts. | +1 Wound (vs Evil) per pt. | Cost: 20 XP

The darkness does not merely fear your blade; it fears the light that guides it. You have consecrated your spirit to the destruction of the profane, turning your very presence into a ward against the shadow and a catalyst for its ruin.

You possess a natural resonance that allows you to bypass the defenses of unholy creatures while bolstering your own.

  • Righteous Furor (Offense): For every point invested in this skill, you deal +1 additional Wound to "Evil" creature types by any means (Melee, Ranged, or Magic).
  • Sanctified Bastion (Defense): For every 3 points invested in this skill, you gain +1 Wound Mitigation (WM) specifically against attacks from "Evil" creature types.
  • Designated "Evil" Targets: Beastmen, Chaos Beasts & Cultists, Daemons / Demons, Dragons (Black, Blue, Red, White), Undead.
Mending Power of Three: +1 Charisma per 3 pts. | Cost: Halfling (20), Dwarf (25), Elf/Human (30)

The battlefield is a place of jagged iron and splintered bone. While others focus on the art of the wound, you have mastered the art of the stitch. It is the difference between a clean bandage and a funeral.

Battlefield Triage: You possess the steady hands and medical knowledge required to keep your allies from the brink of death through non-magical means.

  • Stabilization: With 1 point in this skill, you can stabilize an unconscious ally at 0 Wounds, preventing them from succumbing to their injuries for one turn.
  • Bleed Mitigation: Your expertise allows you to manage hemorrhaging. While you cannot remove the "Bleeding" condition itself, you mitigate the damage it causes: the victim suffers 1 less wound per turn for every point you have in Mending.
  • Calculated Healing: Your refined medical skill allows you to restore vitality. Your base Healing value is calculated as: Points in Mending + (Mending Points / 3).
  • Constraints: These actions can only be performed when neither you nor the wounded individual is currently engaged in melee combat.

The world is full of ghosts, lies, and shadows. While others see a wall, you see the faint shimmer of an illusion; while others hear a tale, you hear the hitch in a liar's breath. You do not just look—you observe.

Your heightened awareness allows you to pierce deceptions, whether they are woven from words or woven from raw magic.

  • Discernment: You may roll a Perception test (1d10 + Skill Points) to detect lies or see through storytelling. This acts as the direct mechanical counter to an opponent’s Charm attempts.
  • Arcane Sight: Characters with Druidic, Faith, Shaman, or Wizardry skills may use this skill to detect magical auras, identify the presence of illusions, or interpret supernatural visions.
  • The Marksman’s Edge (Ranged): Your ability to track movement is vital for precision. For every 3 points invested in Perception, you gain a passive +1 bonus to all Ranged To-Hit rolls.
Precise Strike Power of Three: +1 To-Hit & +2 Wounds per 3 pts. | +1 Awareness per 3 pts. | Cost: Elf (40), Dwarf/Human (45), Halfling (50)

A brute swings for the chest and hopes for the best. A master waits for the heartbeat between breaths, finding the narrow gap in the plate where the metal meets the soul. You do not just hit your target; you dismantle them.

The Critical Opening: You have mastered the art of the lethal follow-through. When you roll a natural, unmodified 10 on your initial To-Hit roll, you gain the following benefits:

  • The Lethal Follow-Through: You may add your total points in Precise Strike as a bonus to your secondary "Crit" roll.
  • Armor Shred: If your secondary roll is a success, you bypass the enemy’s defenses. Reduce the target's Armor Mitigation by your total points in this skill for that attack.
  • Compounding Momentum: If you roll a natural, unmodified 10 on your secondary roll, you continue to add your Precise Strike points to any subsequent rolls for that action (though Armor Mitigation cannot be reduced further than its initial shred).

Passive Mastery Scaling

  • Calculated Accuracy: For every 3 points invested, you gain a passive +1 bonus to all To-Hit rolls (Melee and Ranged).
  • Vital Point Targeting: For every 3 points invested, you gain a passive +2 bonus to total Wounds dealt with normal attacks.
  • Passive Progression: For every 3 points invested in this skill, your core Awareness characteristic permanently increases by 1.

  • Opposed Tests:
    - Passive Detection: Enemies use their base Awareness bonus to notice you.
    - Active Detection: Enemies searching for you use their Acute Senses bonus.

Stealth Maneuvers

  • 1. Ambush (The Lethal Start): You strike from a place of hiding or a prepared trap. If successful, your first To-Hit roll gains Advantage.
    - Undetected: Roll 1d10 + Stealth. This becomes the DV for the target’s Awareness test (rolled with Disadvantage).
    - Suspected: Roll 1d10 + Stealth vs. the target's Acute Senses test (rolled normally).
  • 2. Backstab (The Finisher): Attempted when you have surprised a target from behind. On a successful hit, multiply the total wounds dealt by x2.
    - Target Unaware/Engaged: Stealth + Advantage vs. Target Awareness + Disadvantage.
    - Target Aware/Engaged: 1d10 + Stealth vs. Target Awareness (rolled with Advantage).
  • 3. Follow / Shadow: You track a mark through the environment. Initial test is 1d10 + Stealth vs. Target Awareness. If successful, all subsequent Shadowing rolls are made with Advantage as long as you maintain the move action each turn.
  • 4. Hiding in Shadows: Disappearing into darkness or cover. Roll 1d10 + Stealth to set the DV. Opponents must roll 1d10 + Acute Senses to beat it.
  • 5. Moving Silently: Navigating quietly. Roll 1d10 + Stealth against the GM’s set DV. If you fail, nearby opponents may roll against your failed result to detect your position.

Thieving Power of Three: +1 Agility per 3 pts. | Action Cost: 1 | Cost: Halfling (5), Elf/Human (15), Dwarf (20)

  • Agile Scaling: For every 3 points invested in Thieving, your core Agility characteristic permanently increases by 1.
  • General Test Formula: Thieving tests are typically rolled as: 1d10 + Points in Thieving + Agility Bonus.

Thieving Maneuvers

  • 1. Dirty Card / Poker Player: The art of the cheat and the read. You may use this skill to manipulate games of chance or detect when others are cheating. Rolled as an opposed test against the opponent’s Intellect (to see through the cheat) or Awareness (to catch the physical sleight of hand).
  • 2. Pick Lock / Pick Pocket: Removing barriers and redistributing wealth.
    - Pick Lock: Tested against a Difficulty Value (DV) set by the complexity of the mechanism.
    - Pick Pocket: An opposed test against the target's Awareness. If successful, the item is removed without the target noticing. If the roll fails by 3 or more, the target immediately realizes the attempt.
  • 3. Disarm / Set Trap: The difference between a safe corridor and a lethal end.
    - Disarm: Requires a successful test against the trap's DV. Failing by 5 or more immediately triggers the trap on the character.
    - Set Trap: Allows the character to set a hidden hazard. The result of your Thieving roll becomes the DV for anyone attempting to spot or disarm the trap.

The Cost of Failure

  • Minor Failure (Failed by 1–2 points): The lock becomes stubborn. You may attempt to pick it again, but the DV increases by +2 as the pins become slightly jammed or misaligned.
  • Major Failure (Failed by 3+ points): The mechanism is completely locked. You cannot attempt to pick this specific lock again until your Thieving skill increases or you acquire superior "Masterwork" tools.
